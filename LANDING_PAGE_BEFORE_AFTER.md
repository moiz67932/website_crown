<h2>Understanding Condo Ownership</h2>

<p>Owning a condo differs from single-family homeownership in several key ways. HOA fees cover building insurance, exterior maintenance, common area upkeep, and often amenities like pools or gyms. These fees vary widely based on building age, amenities, and services—older buildings may have higher costs due to deferred maintenance, while luxury towers with doormen and rooftop decks command premium fees. Buyers should review HOA financial statements and reserve funds to gauge building health. Unlike houses, condos involve shared decision-making through HOA boards, which set rules on rentals, pets, and renovations. This means less autonomy but also less individual responsibility for major repairs. Condo insurance (HO-6 policy) covers your unit's interior, while the HOA's master policy handles the building structure. Financially, condos often have lower entry costs than single-family homes, making them accessible for first-time buyers or those wanting to live in prime urban locations without stretching their budget too far.</p>

<h2>Investment Potential</h2>

<p>San Francisco condos attract real estate investors due to strong rental demand driven by the city's tech sector, universities, and tourism industry. Smaller units in transit-accessible neighborhoods appeal to young professionals and students, providing steady rental income. However, investors must navigate HOA rental restrictions—some buildings limit the percentage of units that can be rented or impose minimum lease terms. Appreciation potential depends on neighborhood trends, building condition, and broader market cycles. Condos in emerging areas like Mission Bay or Dogpatch may see faster value growth, while established neighborhoods offer stability. Tax advantages like mortgage interest deductions and depreciation apply to investment condos, though California's rent control laws (Costa-Hawkins) and local regulations can impact profitability. Working with a real estate professional who understands San Francisco's investment landscape helps identify buildings with healthy HOAs, low vacancy rates, and neighborhoods with upside potential.</p>

Walkability scores in neighborhoods like North Beach, Marina, and Mission District are exceptionally high, letting residents handle daily errands on foot.
